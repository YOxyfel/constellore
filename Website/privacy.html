Privacy notice

Effective 22 July 2026. This notice covers the free Constellore browser beta operated by Yane Zhekov under the studio name Oxyfel Games in Bulgaria.

The short version

You can play without giving us a name, email address, password, or payment details. Local practice stays on your device. The optional online beta uses a pseudonymous guest profile for recovery, verified runs, and aggregate diagnostics.

Who is responsible

The beta is operated by Yane Zhekov, an individual in Bulgaria using the studio name Oxyfel Games. The official project website is yoxyfel.github.io/constellore. Until a private support mailbox is activated, non-sensitive questions can be submitted through the public beta issue tracker. Never put a Recovery Kit, bearer token, address, payment information, or other private information in a public issue.

Local practice

The GitHub Pages and itch.io editions keep discoveries, settings, progression, and bounded diagnostic counters in browser storage on your device. They do not upload gameplay analytics, scores, or custom targets. A service worker may cache game files for offline play. You can erase this information from Profile or by clearing the site’s storage.

Optional online beta

If you use the separately hosted online beta, the service may store a generated guest identifier and callsign, a versioned cloud profile, verified run records, leaderboard entries, recovery-code and session verification records, aggregate analytics, recipe ratings, launch-interest status, and security/rate-limit records. The game does not require a real name or public user-written display name.

Gameplay diagnostics are restricted to an allowlist and aggregated. Daily operational analytics are retained for up to 90 days. Completed ranked run state is retained briefly for verification; public scores and account progression remain until the profile is erased or the beta is reset. Server backups exclude bearer and recovery secrets and rotate according to the host configuration.

Why information is used

  • To provide the run, save progress, recover a pseudonymous profile, and verify a result you request.
  • To protect leaderboards and the service against automated abuse.
  • To understand whether onboarding and combinations work using bounded aggregate diagnostics.
  • To investigate a recipe rating or a deliberately submitted “expected this to combine” report.

The production legal-basis assessment, processor list, and private contact route must be reviewed before accounts, advertising, or payments expand beyond this free beta.

Experimental AI

Ranked recipes never depend on live AI. If experimental online generation is enabled and you deliberately request it, the two concepts and a bounded amount of recent game context may be sent to the configured AI provider. The public Pages and itch Explore mode is deterministic and sends nothing to an AI provider. Do not enter names, contact details, confidential information, or other personal data as a word. Generated recipes are provisional, may be wrong, and can be quarantined or removed.

Your controls

  • Export the local or online profile from the in-game Profile panel.
  • Erase the profile through the in-game deletion control.
  • Reset local diagnostics and preferences from the same device.
  • Stop using the online profile and clear local site data at any time.

Because the current account is deliberately pseudonymous, the Recovery Kit is the strongest proof that a person controls a profile. Never send the kit to anyone.

Children, payments, and changes

The beta is not designed to collect children’s personal information and currently contains no checkout, paid entitlement, personalized advertising, or rewarded advertising. A final age-position and Bulgarian/EU professional review are required before commercial release. Material changes will be dated here and reflected in the game’s release notes.
